Football Association Database System

The Football Association Database project is designed to model the complex structure of the football world, including clubs, players, leagues, countries, sponsors, and coaches. It tracks essential attributes such as club details, player careers and transfers, league membership, international representation, sponsorship contracts, and coaching history. With its interconnected relationships, the system provides a clear and organized way to store and manage all major football-related data.

The Relation Model

Before diving deeper, here’s an overview of the key pieces that shape the database. Each card represents an important entity in the system and shows how everything connects behind the scenes.

Club

Stores club name, city, year, league & country.
Connected to players, coaches & sponsors.

Player

Contains full player profile: name, age, position, nationality & kit number.
Connected to transfers.

Transfer

Tracks player movement between clubs, storing transfer fees, contract details, and important dates.

League

Shows the league name, number of teams, and the country it belongs to, giving a clear overview of its structure.

Sponsors

Many-to-many model: sponsors can support players, AND clubs with contract details.

Coaches

Tracks coaching careers across multiple clubs with personal info & assignments.

Country

Represents each nation's name, ranking, and ID, and links players and leagues to their home country.

Example SQL Queries

Below are a few sample queries that demonstrate how data is retrieved and connected inside the Football Association Database.

1. List the names and cities of all clubs in the country with ID 74, excluding FC Koln.


                SELECT CB.NAME, CB.CITY
                FROM CLUB CB, COUNTRY C
                WHERE C.COUNTRY_ID = CB.COUNTRY_ID AND C.COUNTRY_ID = 74
                AND CB.NAME <> 'FC KOLN';

2. List the first and last names of all players, along with their country (matching players to their country).


                SELECT FNAME, LNAME
                FROM PLAYER
                INNER JOIN COUNTRY
                ON PLAYER.COUNTRY_ID = COUNTRY.COUNTRY_ID;

3. List the first name, last name, and jersey number of all players along with their country.


                SELECT FNAME, LNAME, KIT_NO
                FROM PLAYER
                INNER JOIN COUNTRY
                ON PLAYER.COUNTRY_ID = COUNTRY.COUNTRY_ID;
